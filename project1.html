Biography

Linus Torvals is a Finnish-American software engineer who is the creator and principal developer of the Linux kernel for a number of operting systems.He also created the version control system Git. Torvalds was born in December 28,1969 in Helsinki, Finland. He studied computer science at the University of Helsinki, and he developed the Linux kernel as a student project in 1991. Since then, the Linux kernel has become one of the most widely used kernels in the world, powering everything from smartphone to supercomputers.

Achievements

  • 1991-Created the Linux kernel and the versionn control system Git.
  • 2004-Named one of the "100 Most Influential People in the World" by Time magazine in 2004
  • 2010-Receive an honorary doctorate from the University of Helsinki in 2010
  • 2010-Received the 2010 Meillennium Technology Prize and the 2012 Free Software Foundation's Award for the Advancement of Free Software
  • 2013-Inducted into the Internet Hall of Fame of the Computer History Museum in 2014
